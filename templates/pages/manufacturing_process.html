Wooden Frame

Traditional vertical loom structure

Warp Threads

Cotton or hemp threads stretched vertically

Unique to Tibet

Vertical looms differ from Persian horizontal looms

Manual Tension

05
Core Technique

Tibetan Knotting

The Tibetan Loop Technique

Unique method that creates durable, soft pile

Rod Placement

Metal rod placed before warp threads

Loop Creation

Yarn looped around warp threads and rod

Rod Removal

Rod removed after row completion

Cutting Loops

Loops cut to create soft pile surface

Faster Technique

Tibetan knotting is faster than Turkish or Persian methods while creating superior pile quality.

Durable & Soft

Creates a thick, durable pile with unique loftiness and superior softness that lasts generations.

06
Strength

Securing the Knots

Weft Insertion

Horizontal thread inserted after each knot row

Beating Process

Heavy comb compresses knots for maximum strength

Structural Integrity

Creates dense, durable carpet that withstands generations

Precision

Trimming & Carving

Surface Shearing

Even shearing creates uniform pile height

Contour Carving

Artisans carve around motifs for depth and clarity

This meticulous step transforms the woven carpet into a work of art, revealing the intricate patterns and adding three-dimensional depth.

Washing & Final Touches

Natural Cleansing

Natural Washing

Soap and water cleansing, beaten on rocks to remove fibers

Sun Drying

Stretched on frames and naturally sun-dried

Edge Binding

Edges bound, fringe trimmed, imperfections repaired
